Waste water detection

In water quality analysis, numerous parameters are essential to consider, but we will focus on six particularly crucial ones, as specified by the WHO guidelines. Firstly, we will measure the water's pH value, a vital indicator of acidity or alkalinity. Next, we'll assess the water's turbidity, which affects its clarity and impurity levels. Additionally, monitoring the water level in the tank ensures a stable and reliable supply. Understanding the temperature and humidity of the surroundings helps in comprehending the overall environmental impact. Moreover, analyzing the dissolved oxygen content is crucial for aquatic life support. Lastly, measuring conductivity aids in gauging the water's ability to conduct electricity, providing insights into its mineral content. By diligently examining these parameters, we aim to ensure water safety and quality for various applications.

Parkinson's disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive disorder that affects the nervous system and the parts of the body controlled by the nerves, resulting in tremors and slowing in movement, I harnessed the power of TensorFlow Decision Forests to develop a robust model for predicting Parkinson's disease progression. This project involved the integration of two critical data sources: NPX (protein data) and peptides data. By amalgamating these datasets, the model's predictive capabilities were significantly enhanced, enabling more precise predictions of patient Unified Parkinson's Disease Rating Scale (UPDRS) scores.
